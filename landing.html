Shinescreen

A Novel, Entirely Reef-Safe Probiotic Sunscreen

The St Andrews iGEM team has desiged a novel biobrick gene circuit and considered the composition of skin bacteria to achieve a fine-tuned response by our “Shinescreen” - an entirely reef-safe probiotic sunscreen.

St Andrews iGEM 2020

Our project Shinescreen focuses on engineering a novel and sustainable sunscreen using synthetic biology. We are taking the first steps to design a bacterial sunscreen which offers long term protection and is entirely coral friendly!

Research Design

Design a novel biobrick gene circuit and consider the composition of skin bacteria to achieve a fine-tuned response by our “Shinescreen”.

Conserve and Revitalise Coral Reefs

Oxybenzone and octinoxate in chemical sunscreens cause coral bleaching, impair polyp growth and increase their susceptibility to viruses.

Probiotic Sunscreen

We aim to create a smart sunscreen which uses bacteria as a source of sustainable and affordable UV protection.

WHY Engineer a Reef-Safe Sunscreen?

Corals support an impressive 25% of all marine species, providing food, shelter and protection from the open ocean. Despite this incredibly vital role, coral reefs are declining due to human-induced climate change and coral pollution.

One thing that contributes to coral pollution is the use of conventional sunscreens which contain marine toxins such as oxybenzone, octinoxate and zinc oxide nanoparticles.

When a person wearing sunscreen enters the sea, these compounds leach into the surrounding water and at toxic levels, interfere with coral growth and reproduction and increase viral susceptibility. Combined, these effects contribute to coral bleaching and reef decline.

WHY Probiotic?

To combat the problem, we are using synthetic biology to produce the first sustainable probiotic sunscreen. As our sunscreen is a probiotic rather than a chemical-UV filter, it will not degrade into harmful by-products which contribute to skin cancer and damage coral reefs.

The DNA from an aquatic micro-organism will be taken and inserted into a harmless strain of E. coli allowing the bacteria to produce a non-toxic UV-absorbing compound called shinorine in response to UV light.

When applied to your skin, the bacteria will act as a bioactive shield against this harmful radiation.

By responding to UV in this way, our bacteria would offer prolonged protection compared to both conventional and mineral sunscreens as shinorine is only produced when required.


The idea of applying bacteria to your skin is a bit unusual but it is important to remember that probiotics have been around for centuries and common supplements enhancing human gut bacteria are widely available.

In fact, we have more bacteria than our own cells; bacteria are almost everywhere on the human body! Collectively, they are the microbiome – a continually evolving community changed by diet, exercise and showering!

As the application of our probiotic is only temporary, the long-term survival of our bacterium must be prevented. For example, the bacteria in Actimel, are naturally outcompeted by other gut flora. The bacteria in our sunscreen only remain viable for a certain duration of time. Escape into the environment or permanent integration into the microbiome will therefore be prevented.

So, in summary, our bacteria will act as a smart, sustainable, sunscreen alternative offering prolonged UV protection. Using genetic material from aquatic bacteria, we will harness millions of years of evolution to naturally reduce the rate of skin ageing and the risk of skin cancer.
